Tor is Peace, Software Freedom is Slavery, Wikipedia is Truth: The Political Philosophy of the Internet

Apart from further demonstrating my ability to write ridiculously long titles, the talk I gave at the 27th Chaos Communication Congress was my attempt to combine what I knew about political philosophy and FLOSS (Free/Libre/Open-Source Software).

The two have superficial similarites, like their focus on the names of things ("Don't call us political theorists, we're political philosophers"). My aim was to use the methods and theories of political philosophy to a problem of real practical importance to FLOSS groups: do so-called "restrictive" FLOSS licenses really impose more restrictions on freedom?

Because I was presenting to an audience of non-philosophers, I omitted some detail of the academic debate, and included quite a few hacker references that were not stricly necessary (but everyone loves xkcd comics!).
