Hi!

I'm Adam Obeng.

I like understanding things.

Often the best way to understand things is to take them apart and put them back together again.

Unfortunately, human societies are more complicated than your parents' alarm clock.

So I make models of societies instead.

It's easier in the long run, and you don't have to scrabble round on the floor for hours trying to find the bit that went sproing.

This website is supposed to give some examples of the things I get up to — sociological and otherwise. The projects here presented are of varying substantive importance and at different stages of completion, but they should give you a good cross-sectional overview of the types of thing that interest me.

Some of these projects are silly, some are serious, some are academic and some are practical. Throughout, you should get an impression of the kind of person I am (interested in anything interesting), as well as the kind of work I do (anything interesting). At the moment, I'm waxing sociological, mostly because I'm fascinated by the abstract models used in social network analysis and agent-based modelling. But I'm also interested in the philosophy of FLOSS, the digital humanities, making everyday things slightly easier and, of course, my self.

Some of this information is presented in a more formal manner on my CV. If you'd like even more information than that, feel free to contact me.
